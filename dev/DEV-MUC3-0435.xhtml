MOSCOW (USSR), 6 SEP 89 (INTERNATIONAL SERVICE) -- [TEXT] THREE STRONG EXPLOSIONS OCCURRED LAST NIGHT IN MEDELLIN, A DRUG TRAFFICKING CENTER IN COLOMBIA.

AT LEAST FIVE PEOPLE, INCLUDING JOURNALISTS AND A TELEVISION CAMERA CREW, WERE WOUNDED. TWO BANK BUILDINGS AND A RESTAURANT WERE SERIOUSLY DAMAGED.

THE EXPLOSIONS OCCURRED FOLLOWING THE TELEVISED SPEECH BY U.S. PRESIDENT BUSH ON HIS NEW ANTINARCOTICS PLAN.
