GUATEMALA CITY, 19 MAY 90 (DPA) -- [TEXT] THE BUS STRIKE IN GUATEMALA CITY CONTINUED FOR THE FOURTH DAY TODAY. LAST NIGHT, THERE WERE NEW DISTURBANCES AND TERRORIST BOMBS WERE SET OFF.

ALTHOUGH THE SITUATION HAS CAUSED GREAT DISCONTENT AMONG THE 1.2 MILLION PEOPLE OF THE CAPITAL, THE GOVERNMENT HAS FOR THE TIME BEING REJECTED THE MUNICIPALITY'S REQUEST TO PLACE THE BUS SERVICES UNDER MILITARY CONTROL.

THE BUS OWNERS ARE ASKING FOR A 100-PERCENT INCREASE IN THE 20 CENTAVO ($.05) FARE, ARGUING THAT INFLATION HAS INCREASED THE PRICE OF GASOLINE AND THE BUS MAINTENANCE COSTS. HOWEVER, THE MUNICIPALITY HAS NOT AUTHORIZED THE INCREASE, FEARING A GENERALIZED PROTEST BY BUS PASSENGERS.
