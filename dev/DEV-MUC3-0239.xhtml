SAN JOSE (COSTA RICA), 9 JUN 89 (ACAN-EFE) -- [TEXT] COSTA RICAN PRESIDENT OSCAR ARIAS SANCHEZ HAS SAID HE IS "ALARMED" OVER THE ASSASSINATION OF ANTONIO RODRIGUEZ PORTH, SALVADORAN MINISTER OF THE PRESIDENCY, BY SUSPECTED URBAN GUERRILLAS EARLY THIS MORNING IN SAN SALVADOR.

ARIAS TOLD ACAN-EFE THAT HE FEARS "INCREASED VIOLENCE" IN THAT COUNTRY.

RODRIGUEZ PORTH, AN INFLUENTIAL LEADER OF THE RULING NATIONALIST REPUBLICAN ALLIANCE, IS THE FIRST MINISTER TO BE ASSASSINATED BY THE GUERRILLAS SINCE THE SALVADORAN CONFLICT BEGAN ALMOST 10 YEARS AGO.

RODRIGUEZ PORTH, 65, WAS KILLED AT 0815 (1415 GMT) NEAR HIS HOME IN SAN SALVADOR, ALONG WITH TWO OF HIS BODYGUARDS, ONLY 8 DAYS AFTER TAKING OFFICE ON 1 JUNE.

FOR HIS PART, LUIS GUILLERMO SOLIS, CHIEF OF THE COSTA RICAN FOREIGN MINISTRY CABINET, DENOUNCED THE ASSASSINATION AND SAID THAT "IT UNDOUBTEDLY MEANS THAT THE SITUATION IN EL SALVADOR IS BECOMING MORE ACUTE, AND IT MAKES AN AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE POLITICAL FORCES AND THE GUERRILLAS MORE DIFFICULT."

ACTIONS LIKE THESE "IRREPARABLY HARM" EFFORTS TO ACHIEVE A NEGOTIATED PEACE IN THAT COUNTRY, THE OFFICIAL SAID. HE EXPRESSED HOPE, HOWEVER, THAT THE SALVADORAN SITUATION WILL NOT DETERIORATE FURTHER.
