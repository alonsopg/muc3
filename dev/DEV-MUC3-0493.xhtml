RIO DE JANEIRO (BRAZIL), 17 SEP 89 (O GLOBO) -- [TEXT] YESTERDAY MORNING, 27 PEOPLE WERE DETAINED AND MORE THAN 30 STOLEN CARS WERE SEIZED IN VARIOUS PARTS OF VITORIA (ESPIRITO SANTO STATE) DURING A HUGE OPERATION CONDUCTED BY THE FEDERAL POLICE DEPARTMENT (DPF). THE OPERATION DISMANTLED THE "ESPIRITO SANTO-BOLIVIA CONNECTION," WHICH CONSISTED OF EXCHANGING CARS THAT HAD BEEN STOLEN IN VITORIA FOR BOLIVIAN OR COLOMBIAN COCAINE. THE "MARSEILLE OPERATION," AS THE OPERATION WAS CALLED, INVOLVED 70 POLICEMENT. THE OPERATION WAS COORDINATED BY THE RIO DE JANEIRO, SAO PAULO, AND ESPIRITO SANTO DPF.

THE OPERATION'S MAIN OBJECTIVE -- THE ARREST OF STOLEN- CAR DEALER JOAO LUIZ DE SOUZA -- WAS NOT ACHIEVED, HOWEVER, BECAUSE, ALTHOUGH HAVING BEEN SURROUNDED BY SIX ARMED POLICEMEN, SOUZA MANAGED TO ESCAPE.
