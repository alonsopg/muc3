MANAGUA (NICARAGUA), 26 NOV 89 (ACAN-EFE) -- [TEXT] NICARAGUAN PRESIDENT DANIEL ORTEGA HAS STATED THAT HE IS "PROUD AND HONORED" OVER SALVADORAN PRESIDENT ALFREDO CRISTIANI'S DECISION TO SEVER RELATIONS WITH HIS GOVERNMENT, AND DESCRIBED THE SALVADORAN CHIEF EXECUTIVE AS A "MURDERER."

IN A SPEECH IN THE TOWN OF SOMOTILLO, ON THE BORDER WITH HONDURAS, ORTEGA SAID THAT THE NEWS OF HIS SALVADORAN COLLEAGUE'S ANNOUNCEMENT "FILLED ME WITH JOY BECAUSE WE WILL THUS AVOID RECEIVING THE PRESIDENT OF A GOVERNMENT THAT MURDERS PRIESTS, NUNS, AND POLITICAL LEADERS."

THE NICARAGUAN CAPITAL HAS BEEN SET AS THE SITE FOR THE CENTRAL AMERICAN SUMMIT SCHEDULED FOR 8-9 DECEMBER.

THE NICARAGUAN PRESIDENT SAID THAT HE WILL ASK THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY TO BREAK RELATIONS WITH EL SALVADOR "UNTIL THE SLAYERS OF THE SIX JESUIT PRIESTS MURDERED IN EL SALVADOR ARE PUNISHED." THE PRIESTS WERE MURDERED ON 16 NOVEMBER.
