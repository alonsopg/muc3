LIMA, 23 MAR 90 (EFE) -- [TEXT] IN VIEW OF THE CONTINUING WAVE OF CRIMES AND TERRORIST ATTACKS, THE PERUVIAN GOVERNMENT TODAY DECLARED A STATE OF EMERGENCY IN LIMA AND THE NEIGHBORING PORT OF CALLAO TO SAFEGUARD THE ELECTIONS SCHEDULED FOR 8 APRIL THIS YEAR.

THE INTERIOR MINISTRY HAS ISSUED A COMMUNIQUE ANNOUNCING THAT POLICE FORCES THROUGHOUT THE COUNTRY HAVE BEEN "ABSOLUTELY FORBIDDEN FROM TAKING LEAVE" [INAMOBILIDAD ABSOLUTA] TO PROTECT POLITICAL CANDIDATES AND CITIZENS IN GENERAL.
